Question 1: Early National Banks

To what extent were the First and Second Banks effective for economic stability? Why did political backlash and regional disputes predict their collapse?

Regulation restrained risky practices and supported growth, but political divisions and lack of consensus led to failure.

Question 2: Inequality & Division

How did early practices embed slavery and regional imbalances, creating lasting political conflict? How were tensions overcome?

20th-century shifts (wars, demographics, new financial system) weakened sectional divides for a more equitable framework.

Question 3: The Federal Reserve

How did the Fed reduce previous tensions with a decentralized but connected structure and independence?

Balanced national oversight, regional representation, and crisis flexibility (e.g., 2008 financial midigation) made it more durable than early banks.
